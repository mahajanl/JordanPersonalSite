I earned a PhD in English from Arizona State University, with a focus in the history and theory of rhetoric. I am currently an Assistant Professor of Communication and English at Mount St. Mary's University.

Research Focus

My research investigates how medieval rhetoricians interpreted the civic aspects of classical rhetorical theory, as well as how their understandings influenced broader social practices related to the formation of civic identity and the maintenance of communities. I focus particularly on rhetoric’s relationship to politics and deliberation, modes of pedagogical instruction intended to shape civic behavior, and strategies of consensus building. In doing so, I seek to articulate not only a more nuanced understanding of the rhetorical theories and practices of the medieval period, but also to interrogate the role of rhetoric in democratic and non-democratic societies. As such, my research considers not only historical cases, but extends also to how those cases might inform contemporary rhetorical theory and education.

Find my Work

My work has appeared or is forthcoming in such venues as Philosophy and Rhetoric, Advances in the History of Rhetoric, Community Literacy Journal, and TRANSVERSE Journal.
